01 // privacy

Nobody In The Middle

Your messages are encrypted before they leave your device. The server that delivers them cannot read them. No company, no government, no one but you and the person you're talking to.

02 // freedom

No Walls. No Gates.

No app store approval. No phone number required. No account to freeze or suspend. Pando runs in your terminal — the one place they forgot to lock down.

03 // connection

Reach Who Matters

Messages wait for your contact to come online. Even if they disconnect, even if the network drops — your words arrive when they return. Reliable delivery for unreliable times.

step 01 //

Initialize

Create your identity on your own machine. Your encryption keys are generated locally and never leave your device.

step 02 //

Exchange

Share an invite with someone you trust. Verify each other's identity through a secure fingerprint before you start.

step 03 //

Talk

Open your terminal and start talking. Every message is encrypted end-to-end. Always. No exceptions.

> /network // how it works

there is no central server.

a relay is just a machine someone chose to run.

it could be a friend. a community. you.

most people just connect to one they trust —
point pando at its address and you're in.

nobody owns the network. nobody can.

and the relay you connect to? it can't read your messages.
it only sees encrypted noise. not words. not names. nothing.

> /signal // why this exists

“the right to a private conversation is not a feature.

it is a prerequisite for free thought.

every message you send through conventional channels is stored, analyzed, and indexed. your relationships become data points. your words become evidence.

pando exists because some conversations are nobody's business but yours.

not ours. not theirs. yours.

we built this for people who need to speak freely to the people they love — across borders, across the distance between what is allowed and what is right.”
